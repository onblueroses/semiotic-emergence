# 100k Generation Run at 8x Scale - 2026-03-09

Seed 42, 8x parameters with evolvable hidden layer (4-16 neurons).
This is the first large-scale run after parameterizing the simulation for CLI-configurable scale.

## Data Files

| File | Description |
|------|-------------|
| `output.csv` | Per-generation metrics, 100k gens (18 cols) |
| `trajectory.csv` | Signal-context matrix evolution, 100k gens (20 cols) |
| `input_mi.csv` | Per-input MI values, 100k gens (17 cols) |

## Parameters

```
Grid:           56x56 toroidal
Population:     384 prey, 16 predators
Brain:          16 inputs -> 4-16 hidden (evolvable, tanh) -> 8 outputs
                MAX_GENOME_LEN=408 (sized for MAX_HIDDEN=16)
                hidden_size gene: heritable, 5% mutation rate, +/-1
Symbols:        3 (u8 0-2)
Vision:         11.2 cells (4.0 * 56/20)
Signal range:   22.4 cells (8.0 * 56/20)
Pred speed:     8 cells/tick (round(3 * 56/20))
Ticks/gen:      500
Elites:         64 (384/6)
Mutation:       Gaussian sigma=0.1
Energy:         start 1.0, drain = 0.0008 + hidden_size * 0.0002 per tick
Signal cost:    0.01 per emission
Food:           200 items, +0.3 energy each (cap 1.0)
```

### Phase 1: Rapid adaptation (gen 0-5000)

| Avg fitness | 18.0 | 26.7 | +48% |

The only period of genuine evolutionary progress. Fitness jumps 50%, brain size drops from 6 to 4.5, and the semiotic metrics all activate. By gen 1000, iconicity briefly peaks at 0.19 and MI at 0.074 - the highest values in the entire run.

### Phase 2: Symbol consolidation (gen 5000-20000)

Symbol usage collapses from 3 symbols to effectively 1:

| Era | Sym0/gen | Sym1/gen | Sym2/gen |
|-----|----------|----------|----------|
| 0-10k | 1362 | 1901 | 749 |
| 10-20k | 2320 | 152 | 137 |
| 20-30k | 2779 | 55 | 6 |
| 90-100k | 2467 | 0 | 276 |

Symbol 1 was actually dominant early (1901/gen) then went extinct. Symbol 0 took over completely. This is monopoly, not differentiation.

### Phase 3: Plateau (gen 20000-60000)

Everything flatlines. Avg fitness: 29-30. Hidden: 4.4-4.6. Signals: 2400-3200.

### Phase 4: Frozen (gen 60000-100000)

Trajectory JSD drops from 0.035 (70-80k) to 0.0006 (90-100k). Inter-symbol contrast drops to near zero. The semiotic landscape is completely frozen.

## Finding 1: Brain size collapse is the root problem

Evolution favors minimum brains (avg 4.3, min always 4, max 6-7 by late run):

| Hidden size | Cost/tick | Total cost (500 ticks) | Notes |
|-------------|-----------|----------------------|-------|
| 4 (min) | 0.0016 | 0.80 | Comfortable margin |
| 6 (default) | 0.0020 | 1.00 | Exactly starting energy |
| 10 | 0.0028 | 1.40 | Fatal without multiple food |
| 16 (max) | 0.0040 | 2.00 | Fatal without sustained foraging |

The difference between 4 and 6 neurons is 0.2 energy - equivalent to two-thirds of a food pellet. With 16 inputs, 4 hidden neurons, and 8 outputs, the brain has zero spare capacity for independent signal processing. Flee behavior and signal emission share the same 4 neurons, making spandrels inevitable.

## Finding 2: Signals are spandrels, not communication

The distance distribution of symbol 0 emissions:

| Era | Within vision (d0) | Signal range (d1) | Far (d2+d3) |
|-----|-------------------|-------------------|-------------|
| 0-10k | 91.0% | 7.7% | 1.3% |
| 20-30k | 80.1% | 16.7% | 3.1% |
| 50-60k | 71.0% | 25.2% | 3.7% |
| 90-100k | 70.5% | 25.9% | 3.6% |

70-91% of signals come from prey that can already see the predator. These aren't warning calls - they're neural crosstalk from flee behavior activating the signal output through shared hidden neurons.

## Finding 3: Signaling actively hurts fitness

Sender_fit_corr starts at -0.16 and stabilizes at -0.34 by gen 30k. Signaling costs 0.01 energy per emission. With ~2500 signals per generation across 384 prey, active signalers pay a real metabolic tax with no compensating kin-selection benefit.

## Finding 4: Receiver benefit is a spatial confound

Receiver_fit_corr stays rock-solid at ~0.48 for all 100k generations. This looks like "receivers benefit from signals" but it's entirely explained by spatial position: center-of-population prey hear more signals AND have more neighbors between them and the predator (dilution effect).

The clean metric - response_fit_corr (does changing behavior based on signals improve fitness?) - is 0.0 throughout all 100k generations. Zero signal. There is no fitness advantage to responding differently when receiving signals vs not receiving them.

## Finding 5: The JSD gap inverts and equalizes

| Era | JSD no-pred | JSD pred | Gap |
|-----|-------------|----------|-----|
| 0-10k | 0.40 | 0.26 | +0.14 |
| 20-30k | 0.37 | 0.31 | +0.05 |
| 40-50k | 0.29 | 0.30 | -0.02 |
| 90-100k | 0.31 | 0.33 | -0.02 |

Early on, prey changed behavior MORE in safe contexts (bandwidth to react when not fleeing). By 40k+ the gap equalizes. The signal response isn't predator-context-specific - it's generic behavioral noise from receiving any input.

## Finding 6: Symbol vocabulary collapses to monopoly

Three symbols -> one dominant + one vestigial + one extinct:

| Era | Sym0 | Sym1 | Sym2 |
|-----|------|------|------|
| 0-10k | 34% | 47% | 19% |
| 10-20k | 89% | 6% | 5% |
| 20-30k | 98% | 2% | 0.2% |
| 90-100k | 90% | 0% | 10% |

Without differentiation pressure (all symbols have identical cost and propagation properties), the system collapses to a single signal type. Sym2 makes a minor comeback in the 30-80k range but carries no distinct information.

## Finding 8: Fitness spikes are stochastic, not evolutionary

The gen 59999 spike (avg 83.0, max 374) was a single-generation anomaly. Surrounding generations show normal values (~25-30 avg). These spikes occur when predators happen to cluster spatially, leaving most of the grid safe.

| Era | % gens with max > 150 |
|-----|-----------------------|
| 0-10k | 15.2% |
| 20-30k | 32.6% |
| 50-60k | 35.0% |
| 90-100k | 37.4% |

The frequency of high-max-fitness generations increases slightly, but average fitness stays flat at 29-30. The tail gets fatter but the body doesn't move.

## Finding 9: Three metrics never activated

- **response_fit_corr**: 0.0 for all 100k generations. Requires 30+ samples in both signal and no-signal buckets per individual prey. With signal rate being roughly uniform across prey (everyone emits or no one does), there's not enough within-prey variance.
- **silence_onset_jsd**: 0.0 throughout.
- **silence_move_delta**: 0.0 throughout.

These metrics need higher data density or lower sample thresholds to produce any signal.

## Finding 10: Trajectory freezes completely

Trajectory JSD (generation-over-generation change in signal-context matrix) declines steadily:
- 0-10k: 0.0164
- 40-50k: 0.0460 (brief resurgence)
- 90-100k: 0.0006

By the final 10k generations, the semiotic landscape is completely frozen. No innovation, no drift, no differentiation between symbols. The system reached a dead-end attractor.

## Diagnosis: Why Communication Didn't Emerge

The system is trapped in a local optimum where:

1. **Metabolic cost favors tiny brains** - 4 neurons save 0.2 energy vs 6 neurons, but 4 neurons can't do independent signal processing
2. **Signals are spandrels** of flee behavior - neural crosstalk, not intentional communication
3. **Signaling hurts senders** (-0.34 fitness correlation) - selected against with no compensating benefit
4. **No receiver benefit** from responding to signals (response_fit_corr = 0.0)
5. **Symbol vocabulary collapses** to one symbol - no differentiation pressure
6. **Speed mismatch** - predator moves 8 cells/tick, prey 1 cell/tick. Signal range is 22.4 cells, but a warned prey can only move 3 cells in the ~3 ticks before the predator arrives. Warning information is nearly useless given the speed differential.

The core tension: signals can only be useful if receivers can act on the information. The predator is so fast relative to prey that even perfect warning doesn't create enough survival advantage to select for signal quality.

## Comparison with Baseline (48 prey, 20x20)

| Metric | Baseline 10k | 8x Scale 100k |
|--------|-------------|----------------|
| Brain size | Fixed at 6 | Collapsed to 4.3 |
| Symbol diversity | 2 active (sym0 extinct) | 1 active (sym1 extinct) |
| Peak MI | 0.237 (epoch 5) | 0.074 (gen 1000) |
| Semiotic epochs | 5 distinct | None (monotonic decay) |
| Receiver JSD peak | 0.693 (theoretical max) | 0.43 |

The baseline had higher peak MI and showed distinct semiotic epochs because its fixed 6-neuron brain provided enough capacity for signal processing. The 8x scale with evolvable brains found the metabolic shortcut: shrink the brain, save energy, lose communication capacity.

## Next Steps

1. **Reduce brain cost** to encourage larger brains and more complex signal processing
2. **Make signals free** (cost = 0) to remove the metabolic penalty for signaling
3. **Fix the speed mismatch** so warned prey can actually use the information
4. **Create receiver selection pressure** so responding to signals confers fitness advantage
5. **Multi-seed runs** to confirm these findings aren't seed-specific
